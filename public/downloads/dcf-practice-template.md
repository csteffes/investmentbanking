## Tab 1: DCF Walk-Through Practice Script

Practice this script out loud. Target: 90 seconds without notes.

### Step 1 — Concept
"A DCF values a company by discounting its expected future cash flows back to the present.
The idea is that a dollar of cash flow in the future is worth less than a dollar today because of
the time value of money and the risk involved in those future cash flows."

### Step 2 — Project FCFs
"I project the company's unlevered free cash flows over a 5–10 year discrete period.
Unlevered FCF = EBIT × (1 – tax rate) + D&A – capex – change in net working capital."

### Step 3 — Terminal Value
"I calculate a terminal value using either the Gordon Growth Model
(TV = FCF × (1 + g) / (WACC – g)) or an exit multiple (EV/EBITDA × final year EBITDA).
In practice, I'd use both and triangulate."

### Step 4 — Discount at WACC
"I discount all FCFs and the terminal value back to the present using WACC—
the weighted average cost of capital—which reflects the blended required return
across all capital providers."

### Step 5 — Sum to Enterprise Value
"Summing the PV of projected FCFs and PV of terminal value gives enterprise value."

### Step 6 — Bridge to Equity Value
"I subtract net debt (total debt minus cash) to get equity value."

### Step 7 — Per Share
"I divide equity value by diluted shares outstanding to get equity value per share."
